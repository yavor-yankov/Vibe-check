### r/startups post (value-first framing)
```
Title: The question that kills most startup ideas (before you waste 6 months finding out)

"Why can't the market leader just add this as a feature?"

This is the one question that deflates the most startup ideas — and the one founders least want to ask themselves.

Most ideas die not from execution problems but from assumption failures. The founder never validated that the problem was painful enough, the user specific enough, or the market large enough.

The validation playbook most people skip:
1. Define the problem in terms of user behavior, not your solution
2. Map who specifically has this problem (not "SMBs" — actual named humans)
3. Research what they currently do instead of your product
4. Identify why incumbents haven't already solved this
5. Find 5 people willing to pay for a solution before you build it

I built Vibe Check to automate this through an AI interview — but honestly, doing it manually with a co-founder or advisor is equally valid.

What's the hardest validation question you've faced with your idea?
```
